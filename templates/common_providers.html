Common Types of Mental Healthcare Providers

Psychiatrists*

Common Degrees:
  • M.D., Doctor of Medicine
  • D.O., Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine
  • Completion of residency training in psychiatry
Training & Approach: A psychiatrist is a physician with special training in the diagnosis and treatment of mental and emotional illnesses. They can prescribe medications but often do not counsel patients.

Psychologists

Common Degrees:
  • Ph.D., Doctor of Philosophy in a field of psychology
  • Psy.D., Doctor of Psychology
Training & Approach:
Psychologists evaluate a person’s mental health using clinical interviews, psychological evaluations and testing. They make diagnoses and provide individual and group therapy.

Counselors, Clinicians, Therapists

Common Degrees:
  • M.A. or M.S., Master’s Degree in a mental health-related field
Licensure & Certification:
  • LPC, Licensed Professional Counselor
  • LMFT, Licensed Marriage and Family Therapist
  • LCADAC, Licensed Clinical Alcohol & Drug Abuse Counselor
Training & Approach:
They evaluate a person’s mental health and use therapeutic techniques based on specific training programs

Clinical Social Workers

Common Degrees:
  • M.S.W., Master’s Degree in social work
Licensure & Certification:
  • LICSW, Licensed Independent Social Workers
  • LCSW, Licensed Clinical Social Worker
  • ACSW, Academy of Certified Social Worker
Training & Approach:
They evaluate a person’s mental health and use therapeutic techniques based on specific training programs. They are also trained in case management and advocacy services.

Psychiatric Or Mental Health Nurse Practitioners*

Common Degrees:
  • M.S., Master of Science
  • Ph.D., Doctor of Philosophy in psychiatry nursing
Licensure & Certification:
  • NCLEX, National Council Licensure Examination
  • PMHNP-BC, Board Certification in psychiatric nursing through the American Academy of Nurses Credentialing Center
Training & Approach:
Nurse practitioner with special training in the diagnosis and treatment of mental and emotional illnesses. Can prescribe medications in some cases

Primary Care Physicians (PCP)*

Common Degrees:
  • M.D., Doctor of Medicine
  • D.O., Doctor of Osteopathic Medicine
Training & Approach:
General practice physician that can make diagnoses and prescribe medications. PCPs do not specialize in mental health care but can work together with mental health professionals to determine an individual’s best treatment plan

Family Nurse Practitioners (FNP)*

Common Degrees:
  • M.S., Master of Science
  • Ph.D., Doctor of Philosophy in nursing
Licensure & Certification:
  • NCLEX, National Council Licensure Examination
  • FNP-BC, Family Nurse Practitioner Board Certified
Training & Approach:
General practice nurse practitioner that can make diagnoses and prescribe medications in certain cases. FNPs do not specialize in mental health care but can work together with mental health professionals to determine an individual’s best treatment plan

Psychiatric Pharmacists*

Common Degrees:
  • PharmD, Doctor of Pharmacy
Licensure & Certification:
Training & Approach:
Psychiatric Pharmacists can prescribe or recommend appropriate medications and are skilled at medication management.

Social Workers

Common Degrees:
  • B.A. or B.S., Bachelor of Arts or Bachelor of Science
Training & Approach:
Social workers provide case management, inpatient discharge planning services, placement services and other services to support healthy living.

Pastoral Counselors

Training & Approach:
Pastoral counselors are clergy members trained in clinical pastoral education to diagnose and provide counseling.
*Ability to make diagnoses and prescribe medications
